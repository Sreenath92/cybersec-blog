Cyber Attack Types Everyone Should Know

Cyber attacks are not only technical. Many begin with trust, urgency, curiosity, or a normal daily habit.

By Sreenath V - Cybersecurity Awareness Blogger

Common attack types

  • Phishing: fake messages that push you to share passwords, OTPs, or payment details.
  • Malware: harmful software that steals data, spies, or damages files.
  • Ransomware: malware that locks files and demands money.
  • Account takeover: attackers log in using leaked or reused passwords.
  • Fake apps: copycat apps that request risky permissions or steal logins.
  • Public Wi-Fi attacks: unsafe hotspots used to intercept or redirect traffic.
  • SIM swapping: criminals take over your phone number to receive account codes.
  • Data breaches: leaked personal data used later for scams and targeted phishing.

Best protection habits

Use unique passwords, turn on multi-factor authentication, update devices, avoid unknown downloads, and verify money requests through a trusted channel. These habits reduce the risk from many attack types at once.

Simple rule: when a message creates pressure, slow down. Urgency is a tool attackers use.
