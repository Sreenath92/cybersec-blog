Digital Arrest Scams: Do Not Panic

Fraudsters pretend to be police, court officials, courier teams, or government officers to scare victims.

By Sreenath V - Cybersecurity Awareness Blogger

How the scam works

A caller may claim your Aadhaar, bank account, SIM, parcel, or phone number is linked to crime. They may move you to a video call, show fake documents, and demand money for verification, bail, or clearance.

Warning signs

  • You are told not to tell family, friends, bank staff, or police.
  • You are asked to stay on a video call for a long time.
  • You are threatened with arrest if you disconnect.
  • You are asked to transfer money to prove innocence or verify funds.
  • Documents, ID cards, or court notices are sent only through chat apps.

What to do immediately

  1. Disconnect the call. Real officials do not arrest people through video calls.
  2. Do not transfer money or share bank details.
  3. Tell a trusted person what happened.
  4. Save screenshots, numbers, call logs, and chat messages.
  5. Call 1930 for financial fraud and file at cybercrime.gov.in.
Simple rule: there is no legal process called digital arrest for collecting money on a call.
