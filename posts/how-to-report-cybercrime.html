How to Report Cybercrime and Save Evidence

Reporting is not only about filing a complaint. It also helps banks, platforms, and law enforcement act faster when evidence is clear.

By Sreenath V - Cybersecurity Awareness Blogger

Evidence to save

  • Screenshots of messages, profiles, payment pages, and error screens.
  • Phone numbers, email addresses, social media links, website URLs, and app names.
  • Transaction IDs, UPI IDs, bank reference numbers, dates, and exact amounts.
  • Downloaded files or email headers if available.
  • Complaint numbers from banks, platforms, and official portals.

Where to report in India

In India, call 1930 immediately for online financial fraud and file a complaint at the official National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: cybercrime.gov.in.

For UPI, card, net banking, loan app, or wallet fraud, also contact your bank or payment provider immediately. Ask them to block the transaction, freeze affected cards or accounts if needed, and give you a complaint reference number.

If there is a physical threat, blackmail, child safety issue, or urgent danger, contact local police or visit the nearest police station. The national emergency helpline in India is 112.

What not to do

Do not send more money to recover lost money. Do not trust people who claim they can recover funds for a fee. Do not delete chat history or block the scammer before saving evidence.

Simple rule: report financial fraud immediately. Speed can improve the chance of freezing suspicious transactions.
