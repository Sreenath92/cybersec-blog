Mobile Security Basics for Everyday Use

Your phone holds email, banking, photos, messages, and identity checks. A few settings can make it much harder for someone else to misuse it.

By Sreenath V - Cybersecurity Awareness Blogger

Start with the basics

  • Use a strong screen lock, not just a simple pattern.
  • Install system and app updates quickly.
  • Download apps only from official app stores.
  • Review app permissions and remove apps you no longer use.

Public Wi-Fi safety

Avoid banking or account changes on unknown public Wi-Fi. If you must use public Wi-Fi, use trusted sites, avoid installing prompts, and turn off auto-join for networks you do not recognize.

Prepare for loss or theft

Turn on device location features such as Find My Device or Find My iPhone. Keep backups enabled so you can recover important data without paying anyone or trusting a suspicious repair offer.

Simple rule: if an app does not need your contacts, camera, microphone, or location, do not grant that permission.
