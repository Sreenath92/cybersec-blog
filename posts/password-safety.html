Password Safety That Actually Works

Strong account security is less about memorizing complicated passwords and more about using the right system every day.

By Sreenath V - Cybersecurity Awareness Blogger

Why reused passwords are dangerous

When one website leaks passwords, attackers try the same email and password on other sites. This is called credential stuffing, and it works because many people reuse passwords.

The safer setup

  • Use a password manager to create and store long unique passwords.
  • Turn on multi-factor authentication for email, banking, cloud storage, and social media.
  • Prefer an authenticator app or security key over SMS when available.
  • Change passwords immediately if an account warns you about suspicious activity.

Your first priority

Protect your email account first. If someone controls your email, they can reset passwords for many other services. Give email the strongest password and MFA you can.

Simple rule: one account, one password. Your password manager can remember the rest.
