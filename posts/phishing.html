How to Spot Phishing Before You Click

Phishing is one of the most common cyber attacks because it targets attention, fear, and routine. The attacker wants you to act before you think.

By Sreenath V - Cybersecurity Awareness Blogger

What phishing looks like

A phishing message may claim your account is locked, a parcel is waiting, a payment failed, or a manager needs urgent help. It often includes a link to a fake website that copies a real brand.

Warning signs

  • The message creates urgency or fear.
  • The sender address looks slightly wrong.
  • The link points to a strange domain.
  • You are asked for passwords, card details, or one-time codes.
  • The message has unexpected attachments.

What to do instead

Do not use the link in the message. Open the company website from your browser bookmark or type the address yourself. If the request is about money, work, or personal details, confirm it through a known phone number or a trusted contact.

Simple rule: one-time codes are for you only. Real support teams should not ask you to read them out.
