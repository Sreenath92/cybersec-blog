UPI Fraud: Warning Signs and First Steps

UPI is convenient, but scammers use urgency, fake collect requests, screen sharing, and refund tricks.

By Sreenath V - Cybersecurity Awareness Blogger

Common UPI scam patterns

  • Someone says you must enter your UPI PIN to receive money.
  • A buyer sends a collect request instead of paying you.
  • A fake support agent asks you to install a remote access or screen sharing app.
  • A scammer sends a QR code and says scanning it will receive money.
  • A fake refund, electricity bill, courier, loan, or KYC message pushes you to act fast.

Protect yourself

Your UPI PIN is only for sending money or approving transactions. Do not enter it to receive money. Never share OTPs, PINs, card details, or remote screen access with anyone claiming to be support.

If money is lost

  1. Call your bank or payment app support immediately.
  2. Call 1930 for online financial fraud.
  3. File at cybercrime.gov.in.
  4. Save transaction IDs, screenshots, UPI IDs, phone numbers, and chat history.
Simple rule: receiving money never requires your UPI PIN.
