What to Do Quickly After a Cyber Attack

The first few minutes matter. Act calmly, protect your most important accounts, and save evidence before it disappears.

By Sreenath V - Cybersecurity Awareness Blogger

First-hour checklist

  1. Stop the damage: disconnect infected devices from Wi-Fi or mobile data.
  2. Call the bank: block cards, freeze suspicious transactions, and note complaint numbers.
  3. Change email password: email controls many password resets, so protect it first.
  4. Enable MFA: add app-based authentication where possible.
  5. Save evidence: screenshots, URLs, phone numbers, chats, emails, and transaction IDs.
  6. Report quickly: use official portals and local law enforcement channels.

If your account is hacked

Use account recovery from the official website, log out other devices, change the password, check recovery email and phone settings, and warn contacts not to trust messages from the compromised account.

If your phone is stolen

Use Find My Device or Find My iPhone, contact your mobile carrier, block SIM access, change important account passwords, and report the loss to police if needed.
