WhatsApp Impersonation Scams

Scammers pretend to be family, managers, delivery teams, banks, police, or support staff to create pressure.

By Sreenath V - Cybersecurity Awareness Blogger

Warning signs

  • A new number says it is a relative or boss and asks for urgent money.
  • Someone asks for your WhatsApp verification code.
  • A message uses pressure, secrecy, or emotional urgency.
  • A contact asks you to install an app or open a suspicious link.
  • The profile photo looks familiar, but the number is different.

What to do

Call the person directly using a saved number. Do not reply with OTPs or codes. Do not transfer money until you verify through a second trusted channel.

Secure your WhatsApp

  • Enable two-step verification in WhatsApp settings.
  • Check linked devices and remove unknown sessions.
  • Limit who can see your profile photo and status.
  • Report and block suspicious accounts after saving evidence.
Simple rule: if a money request comes from a new number, verify by voice call first.
